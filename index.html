Oral Health and Cognitive Decline





Oral Health

Dental Caries

Dental caries of older population have always been limited to root surface caries or secondary caries but more recent studies showed a high activity of coronal caries at later stages

Periodontal Disease

Periodontitis is a progressive destructive chronic oral biofilm-mediated infection which considered as a contributor factor for tooth loss and linked to many chronic illnesses such as diabetes mellitus and cardiovascular disease

Tooth Loss

Tooth loss should not be considered as a normal consequence of ageing (Lamster et al., 2016) but more as an end-result of cumulative lifelong adverse oral conditions such as dental caries and periodontal diseases

Cognitive Function

Cognitive Decline

Cognitive decline which represents the normal and abnormal decline of cognitive functions like memory, thinking and problem soolving

Cognitive Impairment

Cognitive impairment can affect older people when impairment interfers with theiir daily abilities
